About Us

Problem

Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata rank among the top 10 noisiest cities in the world. And many more urban spaces in India have high noise levels, often above the limits defined in Prevention and Control of Pollution Act, 1981.

Health Effects

About 100 million people in India suffer from hearing loss. 85dB of sound (heavy traffic) for more than 8 hours causes permanent hearing loss as per World Health Organization (WHO). It also causes serious health problems like hypertension, hearing loss, high blood pressure, insomnia, behavioral and mental problems and cognitive disabilities especially among children.

Our Solution Vision

Our solution aims to provide holistic views of a noise event and understand when sound becomes noise. We are crowdsourcing data on noise events and citizens’ perception of them that will help us gain deeper insights about noise in general and help in developing better solutions. At the same time, we are delivering value by providing a platform for civic engagement where citizens can help the police to monitor and manage sound.

We hope to spark discussions among hyperlocal communities and help the citizens understand noise in terms of metrics that they can relate to.
